# Commercial and Pricing Approach

## Pricing Structure

Northstar typically separates pricing into implementation services, recurring platform operations, hosting, support, optional enhancements, and third-party costs. Implementation may be fixed-price when scope is well defined or time-and-materials when discovery is expected to change scope materially.

## Assumptions

Common assumptions include timely access to customer stakeholders, availability of integration documentation, customer responsibility for legal/privacy wording, timely review of deliverables, provision of test data, access to required environments, and availability of third-party vendors during integration testing.

## Exclusions

Unless explicitly included, Northstar excludes payment processor fees, third-party license fees, legal advice, formal certification fees, large-scale data cleansing, translation services, hardware procurement, and customer-side change management outside agreed training and handover activities.

## Volume-Based Costs

Recurring costs may depend on number of users, transaction volume, storage volume, environments, support coverage, monitoring requirements, and availability or disaster-recovery targets. High availability and strict RTO/RPO requirements can increase infrastructure and support cost.

## Change Control

Scope changes are managed through a change-control process describing requested change, reason, impact on timeline, impact on cost, implementation approach, and approval status. Minor backlog reprioritization may be handled inside sprint planning when it does not affect contractual scope.

## Liability Position

Northstar does not normally accept unlimited liability. Liability terms, caps, exclusions, data breach obligations, regulatory penalties, and indemnities require legal review and must be agreed in the contract. RFP responses should not accept unusual liability terms without legal approval.
