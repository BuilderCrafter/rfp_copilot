# Assessment Client Submission Package Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines what Northstar may need to provide to the client as part of an RFP response. It is synthetic demo content for the initial assessment and should be used to populate the `client_submission_checklist`.

This checklist is not about missing buyer information. It is about the proposal package, evidence, declarations, and internal actions Northstar must prepare for the client.

## Default Proposal Package

For most qualified software-platform RFPs, the proposal team should expect to prepare:

- Executive summary.
- Compliance matrix.
- Response to functional requirements.
- Response to technical requirements.
- Security and privacy response.
- Implementation methodology.
- Project plan.
- Proposed team roles.
- Key personnel CVs.
- Relevant case studies.
- References or reference descriptions.
- Architecture overview.
- Integration approach.
- Hosting and operations approach.
- Support and SLA model.
- Assumptions and exclusions.
- Pricing workbook.
- Commercial response.
- Signed submission forms.

If the RFP explicitly requests any of these items, mark them as `required`. If they are normal proposal best practice but not requested, mark them as `optional` or `conditional`.

## Financial and Credit Evidence

The assessment should create client submission checklist items when the RFP asks for financial standing, creditworthiness, or similar due-diligence evidence.

Possible items:

- Audited financial statements.
- Management accounts.
- Bank reference letter.
- Company credit rating.
- Creditworthiness certificate.
- Financial standing declaration.
- Evidence of turnover.
- Evidence of liquidity.
- Performance bond.
- Parent-company guarantee.
- Bid bond.
- Insurance certificate.

Handling guidance:

- Mark company credit rating as `required` only when the RFP explicitly asks for a credit rating, credit report, creditworthiness certificate, or financial standing score.
- Mark financial statements as `required` when the RFP asks for audited accounts, annual reports, turnover proof, or financial capacity evidence.
- Mark bank reference, performance bond, parent guarantee, or bid bond as `needs_review` unless the finance team has confirmed availability and cost.
- Financial evidence may contain confidential information; legal and finance should confirm permitted disclosure.

Example requested actions:

- "Finance to confirm whether an acceptable company credit rating or creditworthiness certificate is available before submission."
- "Finance to prepare audited financial statements for the requested fiscal years."
- "Legal and finance to review performance bond wording and cost before bid approval."

## Employee Screening and HR Evidence

Some RFPs require personnel screening. These items are sensitive and must be handled carefully.

Possible items:

- Employee criminal background check.
- Employee credit check.
- Security clearance evidence.
- Right-to-work confirmation.
- Identity verification.
- Education verification.
- Employment history verification.
- Professional certification evidence.
- Confidentiality undertaking.
- Code-of-conduct acknowledgement.
- Training completion evidence.
- Data protection training record.
- Security awareness training record.

Handling guidance:

- Mark employee criminal background checks as `required` only when the RFP explicitly asks for criminal background screening, police clearance, criminal record checks, or equivalent wording.
- Mark employee credit checks as `required` only when the RFP explicitly asks for employee credit history, financial probity checks, credit screening, or equivalent wording.
- Mark security clearance as `required` when the RFP names a clearance level, government vetting, national security vetting, or role-specific clearance.
- Mark employee screening as `needs_review` when the RFP mentions personnel vetting, trustworthiness checks, background screening, or due diligence without specifying method.
- Do not promise blanket employee screening without HR/legal confirmation.
- HR/legal must confirm consent, lawful basis, jurisdiction, data minimization, retention, and whether summary attestations are acceptable.

Responsible teams:

- HR for screening feasibility and personnel records.
- Legal/privacy for lawful basis, consent, data disclosure, and retention.
- Delivery lead for identifying which proposed roles are in scope.
- Security for clearance or secure-role requirements.

Example requested actions:

- "HR/legal to confirm whether criminal background checks can be completed for proposed personnel before submission or contract start."
- "HR/legal to confirm whether employee credit checks are lawful and necessary for the requested roles."
- "Delivery lead to identify which proposed team members require security clearance evidence."
- "Ask the client whether a vendor attestation is acceptable instead of individual screening reports."

Possible client submission checklist items:

- Security questionnaire.
- Privacy questionnaire.
- Data processing agreement.
- Technical and organizational measures.
- Incident response process.
- Vulnerability management summary.
- Secure development lifecycle summary.
- Access-control model.

- Backup and disaster recovery summary.
- Business continuity statement.
- Penetration testing summary.
- Subprocessor list.
- Data residency statement.
- GDPR alignment statement.
- WCAG accessibility statement.
- ISO 27001 control-alignment statement.
- SOC 2 control-alignment statement.

Handling guidance:

- Northstar does not claim ISO 27001 certification or SOC 2 Type II certification in the demo knowledge base. If the RFP asks for certification, mark the item as `needs_review` unless verified evidence exists.
- Control-alignment statements are acceptable when the RFP permits equivalent controls.
- Formal security certificates, audit reports, and penetration test summaries require security lead approval before disclosure.

Possible client submission checklist items:

- Pricing workbook.
- Rate card.
- Fixed-price breakdown.
- Time-and-materials assumptions.
- License schedule.
- Hosting cost schedule.
- Support cost schedule.
- Optional enhancements.
- Third-party cost disclosure.
- Payment milestones.
- Change-control assumptions.
- Exclusions.
- Customer responsibilities.

Handling guidance:

- Pricing should not be finalized if critical scope, integrations, data migration, legal terms, or acceptance criteria are missing.
- If the RFP requires fixed price with incomplete scope, mark commercial review as `needs_review`.
- If the RFP requires unlimited liability, uncapped penalties, or acceptance of regulatory fines, add legal review to the submission checklist.

## Submission Checklist Status Guide

- `required`: The RFP explicitly asks for the item or it is mandatory for compliance.
- `conditional`: The item is needed if scope, legal review, buyer clarification, or internal approval confirms applicability.
- `optional`: The item can strengthen the response but is not required.
- `needs_review`: The item is sensitive, risky, uncertain, unavailable, or requires HR, legal, finance, security, or executive approval.

## Severity Guide

- Critical: Missing or unapproved item can make the proposal non-compliant or legally unsafe.
- High: Item materially affects evaluation, risk, pricing, or buyer eligibility.
- Medium: Item is important for quality or completeness but may not block submission.
- Low: Item is helpful or administrative.

## Example Submission Checklist Items

- Company credit rating: Finance to provide an approved creditworthiness certificate or confirm it is unavailable before bid approval.
- Employee criminal background checks: HR/legal to confirm lawful basis, timing, consent, and whether attestations are acceptable.
- Employee credit checks: HR/legal to confirm legality and role necessity before making any commitment.
- Cyber insurance certificate: Finance/security to provide current certificate if required by the RFP.
- Data processing agreement: Legal/privacy to review buyer DPA and prepare response.
- Key personnel CVs: Delivery lead to select proposed team and proposal manager to format CVs.
- Architecture diagram: Solution architect to prepare high-level target architecture and integration diagram.
- Pricing workbook: Commercial owner to prepare pricing with assumptions, exclusions, and optional scope.
