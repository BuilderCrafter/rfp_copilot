# Assessment Bid Decision Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook gives the proposal team a structured basis for the initial RFP assessment. It is synthetic demo content for Northstar Digital Solutions and should be used with the RFP intake policy, company profile, commercial approach, security policy, delivery methodology, and case studies.

The assessment should answer:

- Should we bid for this project?
- What risks would make this a no-bid or executive-review decision?
- Which facts from the RFP support the recommendation?
- Which missing buyer information should be requested before final pricing or contract commitment?

## Target Opportunity Profile

Northstar is strongest when the buyer needs a custom digital workflow platform, secure portal, case-management solution, integration layer, cloud-hosted application, or regulated-service modernization. Strong-fit opportunities usually include several of these signals:

- Custom software, digital service design, workflow automation, portal implementation, user authentication, role-based administration, case management, document upload, notifications, dashboards, analytics, reporting, or business process modernization.
- Integration with existing systems such as CRM, GIS, EHR, core banking, identity providers, payment gateways, document management, data warehouses, fraud monitoring, or public registries.
- Sensitive or regulated data where security, privacy, audit logging, access control, data retention, and governance matter.
- Public sector, financial services, healthcare, utilities, education, logistics, or enterprise operations.
- Buyer willingness to run discovery, confirm requirements, answer clarification questions, and work in a phased delivery model.
- Need for a delivery partner that can manage cross-functional stakeholders rather than only supply staff.

## Strong Bid Signals

The assessment may recommend bid when the RFP shows:

- Clear software/platform scope aligned with Northstar capabilities.
- Meaningful integration or workflow complexity that differentiates Northstar from commodity vendors.
- Security, privacy, accessibility, support, and operations requirements that match existing policies.
- Realistic timeline with discovery, design, implementation, testing, UAT, handover, and post-launch support.
- Buyer values implementation methodology, governance, stakeholder coordination, case studies, and source-backed answers.
- Commercial model can be fixed-price after discovery, phased fixed price, or time-and-materials with clear controls.
- Contract terms appear negotiable and do not require unacceptable liability before legal review.

## Bid With Conditions

The assessment may recommend bid with review or conditions when the opportunity is attractive but incomplete. Common conditions:

- Budget or affordability guardrails are missing.
- Evaluation criteria, scoring weights, award process, or demo expectations are missing.
- Integration inventory, API documentation, data volumes, and source-system ownership are unclear.
- Data migration scope is requested but record counts, source quality, cleansing ownership, and formats are missing.
- Security or privacy obligations are high but required evidence is not clear.
- Contract terms are referenced but draft terms are not provided.
- Proposal submission package includes sensitive HR, credit, security clearance, or financial due-diligence evidence that requires legal or HR confirmation.
- Timeline is compressed but may be feasible if scope is phased.

In these cases, the assessment should produce missing-information checklist items and client clarification questions. The recommendation should normally be `needs_review` unless the RFP has strong fit and the missing items can be resolved through standard clarification.

## No-Bid Signals

The assessment should recommend no-bid or executive escalation when the RFP contains one or more hard-mismatch signals:

- Construction, civil works, building renovation, roads, bridges, concrete, excavation, HVAC, facilities management, hardware installation, or physical infrastructure with no meaningful software component.
- Hardware-only procurement, commodity device supply, reseller-only work, cabling, or equipment leasing where Northstar would not deliver software outcomes.
- Staff augmentation-only request where the buyer only wants individual contractors, resumes, rate cards, and time sheets, with no accountable delivery scope.
- Manufacturing, warehousing, event staffing, logistics operations, call-center outsourcing, or physical product supply without software platform delivery.
- Mandatory unlimited liability, uncapped penalties, acceptance of regulatory fines, broad indemnities, or source-code transfer that legal would reject.
- Required formal certification claim that Northstar cannot currently make, such as ISO 27001 certification or SOC 2 Type II certification, unless a human reviewer confirms evidence.
- Proposal requires employee criminal background checks, employee credit checks, or security clearances in a jurisdiction or timeframe that HR/legal cannot support.
- Timeline is not credible for the requested scope and the buyer refuses discovery, phasing, or reduced MVP scope.
- Buyer requires full financial risk transfer without providing scope, acceptance criteria, or dependency commitments.

## Executive Review Triggers

Escalate to executive review instead of a simple bid recommendation when:

- Estimated contract value is unusually large or strategically important.
- The RFP names a new geography, new regulatory environment, or data residency requirement not covered by existing policies.
- Buyer requires local legal entity registration, local staffing, local data center, or sovereign cloud obligations.
- Scope includes AI decisioning, biometric identity verification, sanctions screening, health data, children data, or other elevated-risk data processing.
- RFP asks for company credit rating, bank guarantees, performance bonds, parent-company guarantees, or audited financial statements.
- RFP asks for employee criminal background checks, employee credit history, drug testing, security clearance, or government vetting.
- Buyer requires unusual insurance, liquidated damages, cyber breach penalties, or personal liability for directors.
- Opportunity is strategically valuable but has weak win probability.

## Win Probability Factors

The bid decision should consider whether Northstar can realistically win, not only whether it can deliver. Positive win-probability factors:

- RFP asks for capabilities Northstar can prove through policies, case studies, and previous answers.
- Buyer asks for implementation approach, governance, security, privacy, support, and integrations rather than only lowest price.
- Northstar has similar references or can reuse accelerators.
- Buyer permits clarification questions and vendor dialogue.
- Evaluation criteria value quality, method, experience, and risk management.

Negative win-probability factors:

- Incumbent appears strongly favored.
- Evaluation is price-dominant and scope is generic.
- RFP has highly specific requirements aligned to a competitor product.
- Mandatory local certification, local office, or license is missing.
- Submission window is too short to prepare a high-quality response.
- Required documents cannot be produced before the deadline.

## Bid Recommendation Language

Use concise, practical language in the assessment summary:

- `bid`: "The RFP aligns with Northstar's secure software platform, integration, and delivery strengths. Missing items can be handled through clarification before final pricing."
- `needs_review`: "The RFP has relevant fit signals, but material information or due-diligence requirements require commercial, legal, HR, or executive review before committing proposal effort."
- `no_bid`: "The RFP is outside Northstar's target services or contains unacceptable delivery, legal, commercial, or evidence requirements."

## Evidence Expectations

Every bid factor should cite short evidence from the RFP or knowledge base. Avoid unsupported claims such as "we are certified" unless the knowledge base explicitly states verified certification. If evidence is weak, mark the factor neutral or needs review.
