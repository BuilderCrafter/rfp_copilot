# Accessibility and UX Standards

## Accessibility Commitment

Northstar designs public-facing services to support accessible use by people with different abilities, devices, and technical skill levels. For public-sector and healthcare projects, Northstar normally targets WCAG 2.2 AA unless the contract specifies a different standard.

## Design Practices

Design practices include semantic HTML, keyboard navigation, visible focus states, sufficient color contrast, clear form labels, meaningful error messages, responsive layouts, and avoidance of interaction patterns that require precise pointer control.

## Content and Localization

Content should use plain language and consistent terminology. The platform can support multilingual content through configurable text resources or integration with content management tools. Translation quality and legal wording normally remain customer responsibilities unless translation services are included.

## Testing

Accessibility validation can include automated checks, manual keyboard testing, screen reader spot checks, color contrast review, and user testing with representative users. Automated checks are useful but do not replace human review.

## Usability Testing

Usability testing uses realistic scenarios and representative user groups. Findings are prioritized based on severity, frequency, and business impact. For citizen or patient portals, Northstar recommends testing with users who vary in digital confidence and accessibility needs.
