# Company Profile and Capabilities

## Company Overview

Northstar Digital Solutions designs and implements secure digital workflow platforms for public-sector, financial-services, healthcare, and enterprise customers. The company specializes in portals, case-management workflows, integration layers, cloud-hosted applications, and modernization of manual or paper-heavy business processes.

Northstar normally delivers projects with cross-functional teams that include a delivery lead, business analyst, solution architect, backend engineers, frontend engineers, QA engineer, DevOps engineer, and part-time security/privacy advisor. For larger programs, the company adds a project manager, technical writer, and training lead.

## Core Capabilities

The company has reusable accelerators for account registration, role-based administration, configurable workflow states, notifications, document upload, audit logging, API integration, dashboards, and reporting. These accelerators are adapted per client rather than sold as a fixed off-the-shelf product.

Northstar's delivery model is best suited for projects where the buyer needs a tailored workflow platform with strong integration and governance needs. The company does not provide payment processing itself but can integrate with payment gateways selected by the customer.

## Responsible Claims

Northstar is not currently claiming ISO 27001 certification or SOC 2 Type II certification in this demo knowledge base. The company maintains controls aligned with ISO 27001 and SOC 2 principles, including access management, change control, vulnerability management, incident response, backup, logging, and supplier review. Any formal certification claim must be verified by a human reviewer before submission.

## Stakeholder Collaboration

Northstar uses structured workshops to bring business owners, IT, legal, compliance, privacy, security, and operational stakeholders into the same decision process. Decisions, risks, assumptions, and open questions are recorded in a shared delivery log. When requirements conflict, the delivery lead escalates to a steering group with options and trade-offs rather than making unilateral scope decisions.
