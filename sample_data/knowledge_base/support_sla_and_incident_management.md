# Support, SLA, and Incident Management

## Support Model

Northstar provides post-launch support through a support desk and named delivery contacts. Standard support covers incident triage, defect investigation, operational questions, minor configuration assistance, and coordination with hosting or integration providers.

## Support Hours

Standard support hours are business days from 09:00 to 17:00 in the agreed customer time zone, excluding public holidays. Extended support or 24/7 coverage can be offered as an optional commercial add-on if required by the RFP.

## Severity Levels

Severity 1 means production service is unavailable or a critical business process cannot operate for most users. Severity 2 means major functionality is impaired with no practical workaround. Severity 3 means limited functionality is affected or a workaround exists. Severity 4 covers questions, minor defects, and low-impact requests.

## Response Targets

Typical initial response targets are 1 hour for Severity 1 during agreed support coverage, 4 business hours for Severity 2, 1 business day for Severity 3, and 2 business days for Severity 4. Final SLA commitments depend on the signed support agreement.

## Escalation

Incidents can be escalated from support analyst to engineering, DevOps, delivery lead, and management depending on impact. Client communication includes acknowledgement, current impact, workaround if available, next update time, resolution, and post-incident summary for major incidents.

## Incident Communications

For high-severity incidents, Northstar provides regular updates until service is restored. After resolution, a post-incident review can document timeline, root cause, corrective actions, and prevention measures.

## Maintenance

Planned maintenance is communicated in advance where possible. Releases include release notes and rollback plans. Emergency maintenance may be required for critical security fixes or severe production defects.
