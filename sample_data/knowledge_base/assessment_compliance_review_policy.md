# Assessment Compliance Review Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines how Northstar checks whether an RFP response is ready for submission. It is synthetic demo content for the shared knowledge base and supports the compliance check stage of the RFP lifecycle.

Compliance review asks:

- Did we answer every extracted requirement?
- Did a human reviewer approve or edit every answer that will be submitted?
- Are risky or unsupported claims flagged?
- Are required client clarification items resolved or explicitly accepted as risks?
- Are required proposal-package deliverables tracked?
- Is the response package ready for final submission control?

## Submission Gate Rules

An RFP response is not ready for submission when any of these blocking issues remain:

- No RFP document has been uploaded.
- Requirements have not been extracted from the RFP.
- Any extracted requirement has no answer.
- Any answer is still only a draft.
- Any answer is flagged for review.
- Any answer has been rejected and not replaced.
- The RFP assessment recommends no-bid.
- The RFP assessment still requires bid-manager review.
- Critical or high-severity missing buyer information remains unresolved.
- Required or needs-review submission-package items have not been tracked by the proposal team.

## Requirement Coverage

Every extracted "vendor shall", "must", "provide", "describe", "explain", "include", or equivalent RFP requirement should have one of:

- Approved answer.
- Edited answer.
- Explicit decision that the requirement is not applicable.
- Explicit no-response decision approved by the bid manager.

The MVP compliance check treats missing answers, rejected answers, flagged answers, and draft-only answers as issues.

## Source and Citation Expectations

Source-backed answers are preferred. If a reviewed answer has no citation, the reviewer should add a note explaining why the answer is acceptable. Examples:

- The answer is procedural and based on standard proposal language.
- The answer is a commercial assumption that was reviewed by the commercial owner.
- The answer is a legal position reviewed by legal.
- The answer is a project-specific commitment made by the bid manager.

Reviewed answers with no citation and no reviewer note should be warning-level compliance issues.

## Assessment and Bid Decision Checks

The response package should not move to final submission if:

- The latest RFP assessment says `no_bid`.
- The latest RFP assessment says `needs_review`.
- The latest RFP assessment contains high or critical missing information.
- The latest RFP assessment identifies required sensitive evidence such as employee criminal background checks, employee credit checks, security clearances, company credit rating, performance bonds, or audited financial statements that has not been reviewed by the relevant owner.

## Client Submission Checklist Checks

The proposal manager should track every required or needs-review client submission item. Examples:

- Company profile.
- Signed forms.
- Compliance matrix.
- Case studies.
- Key personnel CVs.
- Security questionnaire.
- Data processing agreement.
- Cyber insurance certificate.
- Company credit rating or creditworthiness certificate.
- Audited financial statements.
- Employee criminal background checks.
- Employee credit checks.
- Security clearance evidence.
- Architecture diagram.
- Pricing workbook.

Items marked optional may strengthen the proposal but should not block submission. Items marked conditional should be tracked until the bid manager confirms whether they apply.

## Compliance Report Interpretation

Use these statuses:

- `passed`: no compliance issues were found.
- `warnings`: only medium or low issues remain.
- `blocked`: at least one critical or high issue remains.

Critical issues usually block submission because the response may be incomplete, legally unsafe, or contradicted by the bid decision. High issues usually block submission until the bid manager accepts or resolves the risk. Medium and low issues should be reviewed but may not block submission when time is constrained.

## Final Review Checklist

Before submission, the proposal manager should confirm:

- All mandatory requirements are answered.
- All included answers are approved or edited.
- Rejected and flagged answers are excluded or resolved.
- Required attachments are complete.
- Pricing assumptions and exclusions are included.
- Legal and security review items are complete.
- Submission portal format and file names are correct.
- Deadline, timezone, and upload responsibility are confirmed.
